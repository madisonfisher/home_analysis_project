Sales Vs. List Price

This graph shows the median list and median sale prices for the top 100 metropolitan areas in the United States. The line through each is a rolling 6 month average. This attempts to remove some seasonality of the housing industry. As you can see the lowest point each year is right after new years. Another interesting observation is that the housing prices skyrocketed around the time covid began. In normal times the list prices overreact to small market changes because people want to get the most money possible. If the market is going down list low to sell fast before it goes down more, or when its going up list it for a lot and see if it sells. However, with the covid spike, the list prices did not increase as fast as the sale prices. This caused many houses to be sold for more than they were listed for.
